# Appendix C: Practice Schedule Templates

These schedules are starting points, not prescriptions. Adjust session lengths and focus areas to match your energy, your goals, and your available time. The most important thing is consistency. Showing up regularly matters more than the specific structure of each session.

---

## Beginner Schedule (Phases 1-2)

**Target: 3-5 hours per week, spread across 3 sessions.**

This schedule prioritizes making sounds and building familiarity with Ableton. Theory and critical listening are included but secondary to hands-on production.

| Day | Session Focus | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Session 1 | **Sound exploration and instrument practice.** Open a new session. Load Drift or Drum Rack. Spend the entire session making sounds, tweaking parameters, and recording short clips. No arrangement. No mixing. Just play. | 60 min |
| Session 2 | **Rhythm and structure.** Build a drum pattern from scratch. Add a bass line. Try to create a 16-bar loop with at least three elements. Focus on getting things to feel right rhythmically. | 60 min |
| Session 3 | **Active listening + application.** Spend 20 minutes actively listening to a reference track (what is the kick doing? where is the snare? how does the bass relate to the kick?). Spend 40 minutes trying to recreate one element you heard. | 60 min |

**Weekly bonus (optional):** 30 minutes of music theory study using Ableton's Learning Music website or Chapter 4 exercises.

**What success looks like at this level:** You can open Ableton without feeling lost. You can build a basic loop with drums, bass, and a melodic element. You are starting to hear differences in frequency, stereo placement, and dynamics when you listen to music.

## Intermediate Schedule (Phases 3-4)

**Target: 5-10 hours per week, spread across 4-5 sessions.**

This schedule balances creative production with skill development. You are finishing tracks now, so dedicated arrangement and mixing time becomes important.

| Day | Session Focus | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Session 1 | **Creative session.** Start something new or develop a sketch. No rules, no polish. Generate ideas. Record clips, experiment with sounds, try things that might not work. The only goal is to create raw material. | 90 min |
| Session 2 | **Arrangement and structure.** Take an existing sketch and arrange it into a full track. Focus on intro, buildup, drop/chorus, breakdown, and outro. Use the techniques from Chapter 7. | 90 min |
| Session 3 | **Mixing practice.** Work on the mix of an arranged track. EQ, compression, levels, panning. A/B against a reference track. Focus on one problem area per session (e.g., "today I am fixing the low end"). | 60 min |
| Session 4 | **Sound design and synthesis.** Dedicated time for learning your instruments. Pick one synth (Wavetable, Operator, Analog) and build a patch from an initialized state. Try to recreate a sound from a track you like. | 60 min |
| Session 5 (optional) | **Active listening and analysis.** Listen to a full track three times. First pass: overall impression. Second pass: focus on arrangement and structure. Third pass: focus on mix and production details. Take notes. | 45 min |

**What success looks like at this level:** You are finishing tracks. They may not sound professional, but they have a beginning, middle, and end. Your mixes are improving and you can identify specific problems. You are developing preferences and taste in your sound design.

---

## Advanced Schedule (Phases 5-6)

**Target: 8-15 hours per week, spread across 5-6 sessions.**

At this level, you are producing release-ready music. Sessions are more specialized and the balance shifts toward finishing, polishing, and developing your artistic identity.

| Day | Session Focus | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Session 1 | **Deep creative session.** Extended creation time. Start new ideas, develop sketches, experiment with unconventional approaches. This is where you push your boundaries and find new territory. | 120 min |
| Session 2 | **Arrangement and production.** Develop a sketch into a full arrangement. Focus on advanced techniques: automation storytelling, generative elements, breakdown design, transition craft. | 90 min |
| Session 3 | **Mixing.** Detailed mixing session. Work methodically through each element. Reference constantly. Check on multiple playback systems. | 90 min |
| Session 4 | **Sound design or sampling.** Build a library of original sounds. Design patches you will use across your album. Process and prepare samples. This is investment time that pays off across multiple tracks. | 60 min |
| Session 5 | **Mastering and delivery.** Master a finished mix. Export in all required formats. Tag metadata. Prepare for release. Or work on album sequencing and cohesion. | 60 min |
| Session 6 (optional) | **Study and cross-pollination.** Active listening to music outside your genre. Read from the books in Appendix B. Analyze an album's sequencing. Watch a production breakdown. This is input time, filling the well so your creative sessions have material to draw from. | 45 min |

**What success looks like at this level:** You are releasing music. Your tracks sound competitive with independent releases in your genre. You have a recognizable sound. You can identify and solve mixing problems efficiently. You are thinking in terms of albums and artistic projects, not just individual tracks.

## General Guidelines for All Levels

- **Start every session by listening.** Before you touch a parameter, listen to what you have. Play back your current project or your reference track. Let your ears warm up and calibrate.

- **End every session with notes.** Spend two minutes writing down what you did, what worked, what did not, and what you want to do next time. This prevents the "where was I?" problem that wastes the first fifteen minutes of every session.

- **One focus per session.** Do not try to write, arrange, mix, and master in the same session. Each requires a different mindset. Pick one focus and commit to it.

- **Set a timer.** Especially for creative sessions, a timer prevents the endless tweaking loop. When the timer goes off, save and stop. Come back tomorrow with fresh ears.

- **Protect your hearing.** Monitor at conversational volume. Take breaks every 45-60 minutes. No exceptions.
