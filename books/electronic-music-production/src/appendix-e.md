# Appendix E: Case Study Index

Each chapter in this book includes a case study analyzing a specific track or album. These case studies are not just historical context. They are practical lessons in production, arrangement, sound design, and creative philosophy. This index collects them in one place for easy reference.

---

## Case Studies by Chapter

| Case Study | Chapter | Primary Lesson |
|---|---|---|
| Daft Punk -- "Around the World" | Chapter 3: Rhythm | Repetition as a structural and hypnotic device. How a single rhythmic and melodic loop can sustain an entire track through careful layering, filtering, and arrangement. The power of restraint. |
| Boards of Canada -- "Roygbiv" | Chapter 5: Sound Design Fundamentals | Lo-fi aesthetics as a deliberate production choice. Detuned oscillators, tape saturation, and analog warmth as emotional tools. How imperfection creates character and nostalgia. |
| Bicep -- "Glue" | Chapter 7: Arrangement | The loop-to-song transition done masterfully. How a simple melodic idea becomes a full arrangement through additive and subtractive layering. Energy management across a seven-minute track. |
| The Avalanches -- "Since I Left You" | Chapter 8: Working with Audio and Sampling | Sampling as composition. Building an entire album from thousands of disparate samples into a cohesive, joyful whole. Creative recontextualization of existing recordings. Copyright and the art of transformation. |
| Jon Hopkins -- "Open Eye Signal" | Chapter 12: Advanced Sound Design | Sound design as arrangement. How a single evolving sound can drive an entire track. Granular textures, filtered noise, and the blurring of rhythm and texture. Tension built through timbral transformation rather than structural change. |
| Aphex Twin -- "Xtal" | Chapter 13: Intermediate Music Theory | Harmonic ambiguity and emotional complexity. How unconventional chord choices and modal mixture create a mood that is simultaneously warm and unsettling. Theory as a tool for understanding intuitive genius. |
| Burial -- "Archangel" | Chapter 17: Advanced Arrangement | Anti-structural arrangement. Mood over technical sophistication. Pitched vocal samples as emotional hooks. Texture as a compositional element. How vinyl crackle and lo-fi aesthetics create atmosphere and intimacy. Breaking conventions while achieving their underlying goals. |
| Four Tet -- "New Energy" | Chapter 21: Making an Album | Album cohesion across diverse tempos and moods. Sonic palette as a unifying thread. Acoustic sampling as DNA. Sequencing for emotional arc. How a consistent processing philosophy ties disparate material into a coherent artistic statement. |

## How to Use This Index

These case studies reward revisiting. Your understanding of each one will deepen as your skills develop.

When you first read the Daft Punk case study in Chapter 3, you understood repetition as a beginner. After finishing the book and producing your own tracks, go back and listen to "Around the World" again. You will hear things you missed the first time -- the subtle filter automation, the way the arrangement strips elements to create contrast, the precision of the rhythm programming. Your ears are different now.

The same applies to every case study on this list. The Burial analysis in Chapter 17 means something different after you have tried building your own atmospheric, texture-driven track. The Four Tet analysis in Chapter 21 lands differently after you have attempted to sequence your own album.

Make it a practice: every few months, revisit one case study. Listen to the track again with your current ears. Reread the analysis. You will find new lessons each time, because the case studies have not changed, but you have.
