# Appendix B: Further Reading

Curated resources for going deeper on topics covered in this book.

## Books

### Must-Read

**Designing Data-Intensive Applications** by Martin Kleppmann
The definitive guide to distributed systems, databases, and data processing. Dense but essential. Read it twice.

**The Site Reliability Engineering Book** by Google
Free online. The philosophy and practice behind operating systems at scale. Chapters 1-8 are relevant for everyone.

### Worth Your Time

**Database Internals** by Alex Petrov
How databases actually work under the hood. Read after you're comfortable with SQL.

**Release It!** by Michael Nygaard
Patterns for building production-ready software. Excellent on failure modes and recovery.

**A Philosophy of Software Design** by John Ousterhout
Short, opinionated, excellent. On reducing complexity through design.

### Reference

**The Pragmatic Programmer** by Hunt and Thomas
General engineering wisdom. Good for early-career engineers.

**Clean Code** by Robert Martin
Polarizing, but useful for thinking about code quality. Read critically.

## How to Use This List

Don't try to read everything. Instead:

1. **Pick one book** from the must-read section and actually finish it
2. **Subscribe to 2-3 newsletters** that match your interests
3. **Follow engineering blogs** from companies whose problems resemble yours
4. **Bookmark documentation** — you'll reference it constantly
5. **Skip everything else** until you need it

The best resource is the one you actually read. One book thoroughly absorbed beats ten books skimmed.

## Keeping Current

The technology landscape changes fast. To stay current without drowning:

- **Set time limits.** 30 minutes of tech reading per day is plenty.
- **Follow practitioners, not influencers.** People building things know what works.
- **Ignore most trends.** 90% fade within a year. Wait for things to prove themselves.
- **Prefer primary sources.** Read the documentation before the tutorial.

The fundamentals change slowly. Master them and you'll evaluate new tools with confidence.
